Hiring that starts with accommodation.

Most job boards hide what support is available until you’re already deep in the process. On Axol Work, Prospects set their accommodation needs during onboarding. Recruiters tag each shift with the supports they offer. The home feed sorts open shifts by overlap — so a badge like 3 of 4 needs met tells you the fit before you open the listing.

Who it's for

Built for people who work differently.

Two account types share one platform: Prospects looking for shifts, and Recruiters hiring inclusively.

Prospects

Browse open shifts filtered by city and ranked by accommodation fit, apply from the listing, track application status, message Recruiters, Express interest in workplaces, and request work-history verification for a Verified badge.

Recruiters

Set up a company profile, post shifts with pay, schedule, city, and accommodation tags, review applicants in one hub, Scout Prospects, accept or decline applications, mark shifts complete, and confirm work-history requests.

The network

Not a LinkedIn-style “Connect.” Recruiters Scout Prospects; Prospects Express interest. Accept to join each other’s network. Chat 1:1 with read receipts and reactions, post to a community feed, join groups, and find peers with shared experience tags.

For Prospects

Find work that fits.

From signup to a verified track record — here’s what the app actually does.

1

Set how you work

Onboarding covers experience tags, city, availability, and six accommodation needs — quiet workspace, seated breaks, written instructions, flexible starts, predictable routines, and lifting limits. Those needs power the fit ranking on your home feed.

2

See shifts ranked by fit

Open shifts are sorted by how many of your needs the Recruiter tagged on that shift. Each card can show a fit badge like 3 of 4 needs met. Filter by city, then open a listing for pay, address, times, and accommodations.

3

Apply and track status

Open a shift, review the details, and apply. Track every application — submitted, viewed, accepted, or declined — from Applications. Withdraw while it’s still submitted, or message the Recruiter from the same screen.

4

Request a verified history

Add jobs you’ve done and send a verification request to the Recruiter on Axol Work. When they confirm, you get a Verified badge. After that, you can leave tag-based reviews about that workplace.

For Recruiters

Hire people who are ready to work.

Company profile, shift board, applicants hub, and verification inbox — in one web app.

01

Set up the company, then post shifts

Company profile covers name, address, and workplace supports (headphones allowed, seated stations, structured training). Each shift you post has its own title, pay, times, city, and accommodation tags — those tags are what Prospects match against.

02

Review applicants in one hub

See everyone who applied across your shifts. Open a profile, message them, Scout them into your network, and accept or decline. Accepting fills the shift. Marking complete records hours worked.

03

Confirm work history

When a Prospect requests verification for work with your company, confirm or deny from the Company page. Confirming grants them a Verified badge and lets them review you.

04

Earn tag-based reviews

Verified workers can leave reviews built from tags — accommodations honored, training was structured, pay was on time, and more — plus an optional short note. Reviews show on your public profile and Company page.

More than a job board

A network that has your back.

Shifts are the core. The social layer is built into the same app — scouting, chat, a community feed, groups, and peer discovery.

Real-time

Scout & Express interest

Recruiters Scout Prospects; Prospects Express interest in Recruiters. Peers Reach out. Pending shows as Scouted · Pending or Interest sent · Pending. Accept to join each other’s network. 1:1 chat includes unread indicators, read receipts, and emoji reactions.

Community

Feed, groups & peers

Post to a community feed (everyone or your network only), create or join interest groups with their own discussion feeds, and use Mentorship to find people who share your experience tags.

Accessible by design

Not a feature. The foundation.

Axol Work is built toward WCAG 2.1 AA practices — because a platform for disability-inclusive hiring has to work for the people who use it.

01

Keyboard & screen reader

Semantic structure, visible focus, skip-to-content, and live regions for messages, notifications, and toasts. Status always pairs text with icons — never color alone.

02

Light, dark & your zoom

Manual light/dark theme with an initial OS preference, large touch targets, and layouts that stay usable when you scale text or zoom the page.

03

Calm by default

Animations respect reduced-motion preferences. Interactive targets stay large enough to hit without precision.

04

Private & respectful

Your email stays off your public profile. Block anyone, report content, control who sees your posts, and delete your account from Settings.
