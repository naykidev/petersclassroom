The problem

Most websites weren't built with everyone in mind.

Tiny click targets. Gray text on gray backgrounds. Dense pages with nowhere for your eyes to rest. Expensive overlays that hide behind a paywall. Most accessibility products assume you will change how you work to fit the tool.

We think it should be the opposite. Software should meet people where they already are: in the browser, on the sites they use every day.

1 in 4

US adults has a disability that can affect how they use the web.

CDC disability prevalence data

  • Unreadable layouts Low contrast, tight spacing, walls of text.
  • Hard-to-hit controls Buttons and links sized for ideal conditions only.
  • Locked-out tooling Enterprise widgets priced out of reach for most sites.

The gap we close

Practical controls on the sites people already visit. No platform switch. No six-figure overlay contract.

Real challenges

Built for moments when the default web gets in the way.

Every feature starts with a person, not a checkbox. Here is how Axol Assist shows up in everyday browsing.

Challenge For students overwhelmed by cluttered school portals

Cut through the noise before homework even starts

Solution Reading mode and visual clutter controls strip dense portals down to the assignment, syllabus, or link you actually need.

Result Less time fighting the interface. More time doing the work.

Challenge For parents struggling to read small text online

Make any website readable in seconds

Solution Larger type, wider spacing, and high contrast apply instantly on forms, medical portals, and news sites without changing how the site is built.

Result Important information becomes legible without asking someone else for help.

Challenge For people who cannot reliably click small targets

Navigate by hovering, not forcing every click

Solution Dwell clicking turns hover into action on links and buttons across the sites you already use, including smarter timing on YouTube.

Result More independence online with less pain, fatigue, and missed clicks.

Products

Two products. One standard.

Practical accessibility tools you can install today. No enterprise sales call required.

Free Chrome extension

Accessibility Surfer

For anyone who needs the web to feel less physically and visually demanding.

When clicking hurts, text is too small, or a page feels impossible to parse, Surfer adds personal controls on every site you visit: dwell clicking, readable typography, contrast adjustments, and reading mode.

  • Dwell clicking & pointer assistance
  • Text, font, and contrast controls
  • Reading mode for articles
Product details
Script or npm

AccessFlow

Visitor-controlled accessibility for your website.

One script tag or npm import adds a floating toolbar with font, contrast, spacing, and motion controls. Settings persist per visitor in the browser. Install in under a minute on React, Next.js, Astro, Vite, or plain HTML.

  • 15+ visitor-facing controls
  • CDN, npm, or React component
  • No backend or account needed

Why Axol Assist exists

We built this after watching students struggle with inaccessible websites before they could even start their assignments.

School portals, news sites, and homework platforms were already hard enough. Then the layout, the tiny type, and the click targets made getting started the hardest part.

Axol Assist exists to close that gap with software that adapts to people, not the other way around. We build browser tools for individuals and embeddable tools for site owners. We test with real users, keep data in the browser, and ship things people can actually afford to use.

Accessibility should feel practical on day one: readable, navigable, and respectful of how people already browse.

Our commitment: build accessibility technology that is usable immediately, privacy-conscious, and honest about what it can and cannot fix.
